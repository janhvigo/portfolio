The Challenge

How can a company make smarter decisions about where to invest its limited marketing budget during a seasonal campaign?

This was the key question driving our summer 2025 analytics project for Company X. The goal was clear: maximize return on investment (ROI) by leveraging data to decide which stores to target in the upcoming campaign.

Our Approach

We started by analyzing the performance of the 2024 summer campaign, using two main datasets:

  • CompanyX_Summer_Plan_24_activated_Stores
  • Store feat.

While the datasets were rich in variables, we focused on identifying features that were both interpretable and predictive of campaign success.

Feature Engineering & Selection in KNIME

To narrow down the key drivers, we used Forward Feature Selection in KNIME, testing various models. These three features stood out:

  • Summer Precipitation Index ‚Äì High seasonal demand in certain weather patterns
  • Number of Campings Nearby ‚Äì Indicating tourist hotspots
  • Distance from the Sea ‚Äì Proximity to beaches influencing consumer behavior

Predictive Modeling: Choosing the Best Tool

We tested multiple models using cross-validation:

Model Accuracy
Logistic Regression 88.8%
Decision Tree 89.4%
Random Forest 92.4%
XGBoost 90%

Random Forests initially outperformed others. However, after incorporating additional variables like Degree of Urbanization, XGBoost emerged as the top model with 94% accuracy.

Turning Predictions into Business Value

Instead of predicting success or failure outright, we computed Expected Profit (E[Profit]) using the formula:

E(Profit) = P(Incremental Sales) √ó ‚Ç¨1,000 + P(No Sales) √ó (-‚Ç¨1,500)

This probabilistic approach let us weigh risk vs. reward for every store and avoid assuming perfect certainty.

Power BI: Visualizing Store Profiles

We exported our XGBoost predictions and used Power BI to explore geographic and demographic trends. The most promising stores shared common traits:

  • Located in coastal or tourist-heavy areas
  • High percentage of students or young adults
  • Strong correlation with urbanized zones

Key Takeaways

  • KNIME enabled rapid data preparation, feature selection, and model comparison.
  • Random Forests and XGBoost gave us predictive power, but combining them with external variables like urbanization maximized business impact.
  • Expected Profit modeling helped prioritize stores based on ROI, not just prediction accuracy.
  • Power BI revealed common traits of high-potential stores through easy-to-understand visuals.
  • GenAI tools added a creative edge, helping translate data into impactful local strategies.
