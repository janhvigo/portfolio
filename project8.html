Digital Entrepreneurship Lab: Minimizing Digital Tools Resistance

Digital Entrepreneurship Lab - Group Project

Defining the Problem

As the part of a Digital Entreneurship Lab that I participated in, we were required to organised ourselves into groups and conduct research focused on the resistance of employees towards adopting digital business tools. We found that the challenge goes beyond lack of training or familiarity — it reflects deeper reluctance to change workflows, low confidence in using new tools, and a preference for traditional methods.

Our Solution

Throughout a duration of 12 weeks, we performed primary and secondary research and used several tools, such as the Fishbone Diagram, to formulate a solution. After thorough research and brainstorming, we proposed a phased implementation strategy, where digital tools are introduced step by step. This gradual rollout creates familiarity, preserves existing workflows, and builds user confidence over time. By easing employees into change instead of overwhelming them, organizations can reduce resistance, improve adoption, and unlock the full potential of their digital tools.
