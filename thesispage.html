Thesis Overview

This thesis explores how media sentiment toward Artificial Intelligence has evolved over the last decade. Using thousands of news articles from the New York Times API, I applied sentiment analysis models such as RoBERTa to evaluate emotional tone and track how optimism, fear, and neutrality have changed over time.

In addition to sentiment analysis, I used XGBoost-based time series forecasting to predict how media sentiment may develop over the next five years. The study revealed optimism peaking in 2018, a decline in 2020 due to ethical concerns, and a slow stabilization thereafter.

Findings

  • Optimism spiked in 2018, often linked to innovation in AI applications.
  • A sharp drop in 2020 tied to growing fears about ethics, job loss, and privacy.
  • Forecasts suggest sentiment is stabilizing near neutral.
  • Public discourse is adapting as AI becomes more normalized.
